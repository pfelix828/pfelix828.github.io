Marketing Measurement Under Hypergrowth

An interactive simulator for marketing measurement challenges at a company growing from $1B to $30B in 15 months

View on GitHub Live App

What This Project Does

When a company grows from $1B to $30B in revenue over 15 months, almost entirely through organic adoption, every standard marketing measurement approach breaks. Attribution models overcredit by 3-9x. Blended CAC looks like $0.62 when incremental CAC is actually $37 — a 60x gap. A naive before/after analysis shows 28.6% lift, but 27.6 percentage points of that is organic growth and only 1.1% is true marketing impact.

This project builds an interactive application that simulates these dynamics and walks through how to measure marketing’s real contribution when organic growth overwhelms paid signal.

Why This Matters

Most marketing measurement frameworks assume you can isolate paid impact cleanly. At hypergrowth scale with product-led growth, that assumption collapses. The organic wave is so large that every channel looks like it’s working — attribution systems assign credit to marketing touches that would have converted anyway.

This project works through the problem from first principles: what breaks at scale, what methods still work, and how to build a measurement roadmap that separates real signal from noise.

What’s Inside

The app has 12 pages organized around a 20-minute walkthrough:

Primary flow:

  • Overview — The measurement problem at hypergrowth scale
  • Measurement Framework — Why standard approaches fail and what replaces them
  • Method Comparison — MMM, incrementality testing, and attribution side by side, with honest assessments of where each breaks
  • Experiment Designer — Interactive tool for designing incrementality tests with configurable alpha, power, traffic splits, CUPED, and feasibility checks against real daily traffic
  • Cross-Motion Analysis — How self-serve, sales-assisted, and enterprise motions interact and cannibalize
  • User Journey — Activation funnels calibrated to real product flows
  • Decision Framework — Business-question-driven recommendations with a phased Day 1 measurement roadmap
  • Executive Summary — Three things leadership needs to know, no fluff

Supporting pages: Growth Simulator, PLG Adoption Model, Dynamic Cohorts, Data & Assumptions

The Approach

Simulation Engine (Python)

The analytical backbone is a Python package with 11 modules and 33 passing tests that models:

  • Growth dynamics — S-curve adoption with organic vs. paid decomposition
  • Channel simulation — Paid search, social, content, events, partnerships — each with realistic diminishing returns
  • Attribution models — Last-touch, multi-touch, and time-decay, all of which overcredit at different rates
  • MMM (Marketing Mix Modeling) — Simulates media mix models with adstock and saturation, demonstrating omitted variable bias when organic growth isn’t properly controlled
  • Incrementality testing — Geo-holdout and synthetic control experiments with realistic power calculations
  • Cohort analysis — Retention curves, LTV projections, and dynamic cohort behavior under hypergrowth

Interactive App (Next.js)

The frontend is a Next.js app with TypeScript, Visx + Recharts for visualization, and Tailwind + shadcn/ui for the interface. Every chart is interactive — you can adjust parameters and see how measurement breaks or holds up under different assumptions.

What I’d Do Differently in Production

  • Real data ingestion — Connect to actual ad platform APIs and warehouse data instead of simulation
  • Bayesian MMM — Replace the frequentist simulation with a proper Bayesian model (PyMC or Lightweight MMM) for uncertainty quantification
  • Automated experimentation — Build a pipeline that continuously runs geo-holdout tests and updates incrementality estimates
  • Causal inference layer — Add difference-in-differences and synthetic control methods for quasi-experimental measurement when true experiments aren’t feasible
